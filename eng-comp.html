Project Overview

- Designed and built a small-scale rescue vehicle to navigate floodwaters and deliver essential aid to two isolated communities, working within 13" x 13" size constraints and a 5-hour timeline.

- Implemented two distinct propeller systems: a balloon-powered thrust mechanism and a elastic pulley system, enabling reliable propulsion through flood conditions.

- Collaborated in a team of four to design and build a flood rescue vehicle within size and budget constraints. Implemented dual propeller systems (balloon and pulley), adapted the design through iterative testing, and successfully completed the prototype within a 5-hour timeline. Demonstrated rapid prototyping, mechanical design, and effective teamwork skills.

Key Contributions

- Was in charge of the dual propulsion system, designing and building both the balloon-powered and pulley-based mechanisms, and iterating on the design based on testing results.

- Adapted the wheel system from three wheels to two after identifying that rotation wasn't needed, making the prototype more compact and efficient.
