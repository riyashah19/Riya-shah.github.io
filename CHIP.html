Project Overview

- The Conductive Hydrodynamic Ink Printer (CHIP) is an additive manufacturing device that uses conductive, water-based nanoparticle inks to create circuits and sensors for a variety of applications. This robot allows the team to explore applications in PCB fabrication, integrated devices, wearable sensors and printed displays.

Key Contributions

- Enhanced key structural components for the Conductive Hydrodynamic Ink Printer (CHIP) using SolidWorks, designing to ensure a precise fit, which enhanced overall mechanical operation and alignment.

- Designed and modelled a PCB mounting enclosure, improving PCB accessibility by approximately 30% and resolving stability issues, securing team approval.

- Applied tolerance analysis and design on SolidWorks components to ensure reliable component fit and system integration.

- Enabled the physical assembly of the system by fabricating and validating multiple 3D printed components to specification.
