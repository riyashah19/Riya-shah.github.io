Flood Water Recycler

Technical Skills

Leadership Arduino Microsensors Python Sensor Integration Rapid Prototyping Sustainable Design

The Problem

During Kenya's rainy seasons, flooding destroys homes, farmland, and infrastructure. Meanwhile, communities across the country face unreliable electricity access, with frequent blackouts limiting daily life and economic activity. I saw an opportunity; what if the destructive energy of floodwater could be captured and turned into something useful? This question drove me to design a system that harnesses the kinetic energy of floodwater and converts it into electricity, thus transforming a natural disaster into a sustainable power source for communities in need.

Solution

I designed a floodwater energy harvesting system that captures the kinetic energy of moving floodwater and converts it into electricity. The system uses a turbine mechanism integrated into water dams and as water rushes through, it spins the turbine, which drives a small generator to produce power. This electricity can be stored in batteries or used immediately for lighting, charging, or running small appliances in off-grid communities.

To ensure the system could function in real-world conditions, I programmed Arduino microsensors to monitor water flow and trigger the turbine only when sufficient force is present, preventing mechanical wear during dry periods. The prototype was designed to be low-cost, durable, and easy to maintain, which was shaped by the resource constraints I observed in Kenyan communities.
